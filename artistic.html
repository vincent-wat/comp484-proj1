Artistic Gymnastics is one of the most celebrated sports at the Summer Olympics, combining strength, flexibility, precision, and grace. With iconic events like the vault, uneven bars, balance beam, and floor exercise, it has produced legendary Olympic moments and heroes.

What is Artistic Gymnastics?

In Artistic Gymnastics, athletes perform short routines on different apparatuses, demonstrating control, power, and artistry. Both men and women compete in multiple events, with medals awarded for individual and team performances.

Olympic Events

  • Women: Vault, Uneven Bars, Balance Beam, Floor Exercise, All-Around, Team
  • Men: Floor Exercise, Pommel Horse, Rings, Vault, Parallel Bars, Horizontal Bar, All-Around, Team

Olympic & International History

Artistic Gymnastics has been part of the modern Olympics since the very first Games in 1896. Women’s events were added in 1928. Since then, the sport has grown in complexity and popularity, producing stars like Nadia Comăneci, Kohei Uchimura, and Simone Biles.

Competition Structure

  • Qualifications, Team Finals, All-Around Finals, and Individual Apparatus Finals
  • Each routine is scored based on difficulty and execution
  • Highest combined score wins gold

Scoring

  • Difficulty Score (D): Value of elements performed, connections, and bonus skills
  • Execution Score (E): Starts at 10.0; deductions for form, balance, artistry, and landings
  • Final Score = D + E

Notable Teams and Athletes

  • USA Women’s Team (Simone Biles, Suni Lee, Jordan Chiles)
  • Japan Men’s Team (Daiki Hashimoto, Kohei Uchimura)
  • China, Russia, Great Britain, Romania

Fun Fact

Who scored the first perfect 10?

Nadia Comăneci of Romania became the first gymnast to score a perfect 10 at the Olympics in 1976, on the uneven bars. The scoreboard couldn’t even display a 10.00, so it showed 1.00!
