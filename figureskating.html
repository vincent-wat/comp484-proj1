Figure Skating is a signature Olympic sport celebrated for its blend of athleticism, grace, and expressive artistry. Although primarily a Winter Olympic event, figure skating continues to capture the hearts of fans year-round with dazzling performances and emotional moments.

What is Figure Skating?

Figure Skating involves individuals, pairs, or groups performing jumps, spins, lifts, and choreographed routines on ice. Competitions are judged for technical skill, musical interpretation, and presentation.

How Yuzuru Hanyu Sparked My Interest

My fascination with figure skating began after watching 羽生結弦 (Yuzuru Hanyu) compete. His performances are legendary for their emotional power, innovative choreography, and flawless technique. Yuzuru’s ability to combine difficult jumps with expressive storytelling on the ice makes every routine unforgettable. Watching him inspired me to learn more about the sport and appreciate the incredible dedication and artistry involved.

About Yuzuru Hanyu

  • Name: 羽生結弦 (Yuzuru Hanyu)
  • Nationality: Japan
  • Olympic Gold Medals: 2 (Sochi 2014, PyeongChang 2018)
  • Famous for: First man in over 60 years to win back-to-back Olympic singles titles; known for the “Seimei” and “Hope & Legacy” programs; attempted the historic quad Axel jump in competition
  • Style: Blends classical Japanese music with contemporary choreography and extreme jump difficulty

Olympic & International History

Figure Skating made its Olympic debut in the 1908 Summer Games and has been a staple of the Winter Olympics since 1924. Legendary skaters like Evgeni Plushenko, 羽生結弦 (Yuzuru Hanyu), and Michelle Kwan have defined eras of the sport.

Competition Structure

  • Short Program and Free Skate/Free Dance for all events
  • Scores based on technical elements and program components
  • Highest combined score wins

Scoring

  • Technical Score: Points for jumps, spins, lifts, and steps
  • Component Score: Skating skills, transitions, interpretation, performance
  • Deductions for falls or errors

Fun Fact

Why is the quadruple jump so famous?

The “quad” is a jump with four full rotations in the air. It’s considered one of the most difficult elements in skating and has become a game-changer for both men’s and women’s events in recent years.
