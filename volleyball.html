Volleyball has been a staple of the Summer Olympics since 1964, offering fans thrilling rallies and electrifying finishes on both the indoor court and the beach. As we look forward to Los Angeles 2028, volleyball promises to deliver world-class athleticism and heart-pounding matches.

Volleyball at the Olympics

Olympic volleyball features two disciplines: Indoor Volleyball and Beach Volleyball. Both showcase incredible athleticism, skill, and teamwork. Indoor games are played in packed arenas, while beach matches take place in sun-soaked stadiums with roaring crowds.

Quick Facts

  • Olympic Debut (Indoor): 1964, Tokyo
  • Olympic Debut (Beach): 1996, Atlanta
  • Number of Teams (2028): 12 men's and 12 women's indoor; 24 men's and 24 women's beach pairs

Rules & Format

Indoor Volleyball: Two teams of six compete on a hard court. The first team to win three sets (to 25 points, must win by 2) takes the match. Each team can hit the ball up to three times before returning it.

Beach Volleyball: Played in pairs on a sand court. Matches are best of three sets; first two sets to 21 points, the third (if needed) to 15 points. Sun, wind, and sand add a unique challenge.
