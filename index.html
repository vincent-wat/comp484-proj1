Every four years, the world unites for the Summer Olympic Games. In 2028, Los Angeles will host the games for the third time, following its previous stints in 1932 and 1984. The city is ready to welcome athletes, fans, and cultures from across the globe!

What Are the Olympic Games?

The Olympic Games are a global sporting event featuring thousands of athletes competing in dozens of sports. Founded on ideals of peace and unity, the Games bring together competitors from more than 200 nations.

Key Information

  • Location: Los Angeles, California, USA
  • Dates: July 14 – July 30, 2028
  • Expected Athletes: 10,000+
  • Participating Nations: 200+
  • Motto: Citius, Altius, Fortius (Faster, Higher, Stronger)

History of the Olympics in Los Angeles

Los Angeles has a special history with the Olympics. The city previously hosted in 1932 and 1984. Both times, LA brought innovation to the Games—from the Olympic Village concept to celebrity-studded opening ceremonies.

More on LA's Olympic Legacy
  • In 1932, LA introduced the first Olympic Village for athletes.
  • The 1984 Games were the first to rely on private funding and are remembered for their financial success.
  • Both editions inspired lasting community sports facilities.

Venues & Infrastructure

Events will be held at state-of-the-art facilities throughout LA, including the iconic Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum, SoFi Stadium, Crypto.com Arena (formerly Staples Center), and more. Many venues from the 1984 Olympics will be reused, emphasizing sustainability.

  • Opening & Closing Ceremonies: LA Memorial Coliseum & SoFi Stadium
  • Swimming: USC’s Dedeaux Field
  • Track & Field: LA Memorial Coliseum
  • Gymnastics: Crypto.com Arena
  • Beach Volleyball: Santa Monica Beach

New and Returning Sports

The LA 2028 Olympics will feature a mix of traditional and new sports, including some chosen by the host city. The Olympic program will include but are not limited to:

  • Athletics (Track & Field)
  • Swimming
  • Volleyball (Indoor & Beach)
  • Acrobatic Gymnastics
  • Figure Skating (as part of the Winter Youth Olympics)
  • Skateboarding
  • Surfing
  • Sport Climbing
  • Baseball/Softball (returning)
  • Flag Football (new!)
